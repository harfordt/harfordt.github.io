Drill

In the ATC, we follow RNZAF drill. The aim of drill is to produce a cadet who is proud, obedient, alert, and self disciplined and to provide the basis of teamwork. You will learn all the drill you need to be able to participate in our nightly parades, as well as formal events such as ANZAC Day and other commemoration events or public displays. We teach drill with and without rifles, and you will have the opportunity to compete against other units for a Drill Trophy. No. 1 Squadron has won this National competition a number of times.

Shooting

Each ATC unit has its own target rifles. shooting You will be taught how to be safe with firearms, and then we can take you shooting on a range. All of our shoots are supervised by Range Safety Officers, the officer staff members who have done a special Defence Force course, and we shoot on 22m ranges with .22 rifles. We have a strong focus on safety. If you are good at shooting, you will be able to participate in local, national and Commonwealth competitions.

Bushcraft

We’ll teach you how to keep safe and have fun in the great Outdoors. Each year you’ll do a minimum of three camps or tramps, from really easy to quite challenging! We have progressive training, so each year you’ll learn new skills. You’ll also have the chance to attend national bushcraft courses with cadets from all around the country. Some of what you’ll learn includes:

  • Clothing and equipment
  • Food and cooking
  • Use and care of tents
  • Abseiling
  • Stretchers and evacuations
  • Emergency shelters
  • Search and rescue

From time to time we run regional camps, which enable you to get to know cadets from other Units in the Wellington Region. Another great way to make friends!

Flying

Over your three years at ATC you will get to experience a number of different flights. Many of these will include you sitting in the pilot’s seat, flying the plane, whilst your instructor assists you. The flights we offer as part of our training include:

  • Introductory power flight
  • Night flying
  • Gliding
  • Aerobatics
  • Cross-country flying
  • Helicopter flying

As a member of the ATC, you will also be able to attend subsidised national flying (and navigation) courses, that can take you right through to your first solo flight in a powered aircraft or glider. Now that’s something most of your mates won’t do!

The New Zealand Cadet Forces (NZCF) is for both boys and girls aged 13 to 18. It comprise the Sea Cadet Corps, the New Zealand Cadet Corps and the Air Training Corps. It is a voluntary, disciplined, uniformed youth leadership training organisation. The organisation is not part of the New Zealand Defence Force (NZDF), but is directed by the Chief of Defence Force, on behalf of the Minister of Defence, and is supported in partnership by the NZDF and the community.

Air Training Corps ATC

We are a youth organisation for students from Year 9 to Year 13. You can join ATC when you are aged between 13 and 15 years old.

The ATC is a voluntary, disciplined youth organisation, with an Air Force feel. We are part of the New Zealand Cadet Forces, which also includes the Cadet Corps (Army Cadets) and the Sea Cadet Corps.

We have a distinct military flavour, from wearing an Air Force Uniform to doing Air Force drill, and this makes us very unique compared to other youth organisations in New Zealand.

Cadet Core CC

Cadet Core Units are an 'Army' Cadet Unit base. Cadets is for teenagers aged 12 - 15 at secondary school, and parades on a week night night at 6:30 - 9:15pm in their Unit every week during the school term.

Cadets participate in a number of activities including practical and theory lessons, tramping and camping and participating in community events such as the 'Around the Bays' run and ANZAC day ceremonies.

Being part of a Cadet Unit can also assist those who are interested in a career in the New Zealand Defence Force, whether the Army, Navy or Air Force. Cadets can help those prepare by teaching them the basics in leadership training, drill and looking after a uniform and giving them the basic experience in participating in military style training.

Sea Cadets SC

Sea Cadets is a training organisation with a military flavour for youth aged 13-18 years. As a part of the New Zealand Cadet Forces (NZCF) we provide a fun and challenging environment for learning new skills and meeting new friends. We have a focus on fostering strong leadership skills and providing many opportunities for youth not available in other organisations.

Some of the activities you’ll get to do with TS Amokura (which is one of many ships that NZCF has) include sailing and powerboat handling, target shooting, visiting Navy ships and bases, overseas exchanges, camping, and participating in competitions against other local cadet units.
