Traditional ✕ Digital

Being a past traditional artist and now a current digital artist, I must say that I'm impressed my sister and I have come this far. At first, we only drew traditionally (with pencil/pen on paper) as we refused to work with a drawing tablet. Despite having a good quality tablet collecting dust (Wacom Intuos4 X), we constantly told ourselves that we were satisfied with traditional art, that we didn't need to start digital art, and that it was 'too hard to learn to use the drawing tablet'. However, over the years we grew tired of only drawing traditionally. We didn't want to stick with pen and paper forever, so we managed to gather up the motivation to hunt down a good drawing program (Paint Tool SAI) and wiped the dust off the unused Wacom tablet. And we were right, it was hard getting used to drawing digitally; not only that, but it wasn't until 2 years later after digitally drawing that we discovered pen sensitivity pressure on our tablet! (We were going nuts about it ahaha) Thanks to that, our drawings got 100% better and it became really easy to use the tablet. Now, we do all of our drawings straight on to SAI, but on the rare occassion, we will draw traditionally first and then scan the image on to the pc.
tl;dr - We decided to switch to digital from traditional and kept it that way.

Below are comparisons between Mikoto's traditional and digital art pieces.
Traditional - My traditional art tends to be rather scribbly and messy. I don't bother using the eraser too much to avoid smudging the lead. Also, when I draw traditionally I tend to have my anatomy proportions slightly off. I always say that the problem with traditional art is that there's no "Ctrl+Z" button~
Digital - In my opinion, my digital art is always 100% better than my traditional; hence why I stick with it now. Digital is much easier for me, and it's always way more visually appealing to look at. I can play around with the colours and brushes as I like; I feel I have more freedom when I draw digitally in comparison to a pen & paper.
